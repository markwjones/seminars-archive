Image Segmentation Models and Numerical Solution Methods

Professor Ke Chen

Department of Mathematical Science, University of Liverpool

Edge detection algorithms were the traditional methods for finding
feature edges in an image but noise can seriously reduce the effectiveness of
such models. More recently, variational segmentation models provide more effective and reliable tools for image
processing applications. The Chan-Vese (2001) region based model in two dimensions is widely used.

In this talk, I shall first present some new multigrid algorithms for this model in the three dimensional setting.
Then I show some recent works for the task of selective segmentation
where pre-defined geometric constraints guide the segmentation models.
Such problems are more common in medical imaging than the usual segmentation.
Numerical experiments will demonstrate the advantages of the new new models over existing results. Collaborators related to this work include
Noor Badshah (Peshawar, Pakistan), Jian-ping Zhang and Bo Yu (Dalian, China) and Lavdie Rada (Liverpool).
